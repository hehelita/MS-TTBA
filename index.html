This demo page compares text-to-binaural audio generation by Stable_audio_open, SpatialSonic and Ours. Using identical text prompts, it provides a fair, side-by-side evaluation of spatialization quality and consistency. Please wear headphones while listening to these audios for the best experience, and pay particular attention to the perceived sound direction!

Additional notes: The demos above show that our method produces a stronger spatial impression than the compared methods. This improvement is partly attributed to our mid–side decomposition. Below, we use mutual information to verify that the side representation is indeed more focused on capturing spatial attributes.

Feature construction

  • Time domain: each waveform is divided into 64 bins. We extract five statistics from each bin (mean, standard deviation, absolute mean, RMS, and peak-to-peak range), resulting in a 320-D feature vector.
  • Frequency domain: we compute a 64-band log-mel spectrogram and perform mean pooling along the temporal axis, resulting in a 64-D feature vector.

MI Sum and MI Mean

MI is calculated separately for each feature dimension with respect to the spatial label. MI Sum is the total MI over all dimensions, while MI Mean is the average MI per dimension.

Domain Representation MI Sum MI Mean
Time Mid 1.333 0.004
Side 7.315 0.023
Frequency Mid 0.843 0.013
Side 1.876 0.029

Side shows a clear advantage in both views: its MI Sum is about 5.5× that of Mid in the time-domain summary and about 2.2× in the frequency-domain summary. This shows that the side representation is indeed more focused on capturing spatial attributes.
